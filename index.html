The Four Great Eras

A timeline of musical evolution and style.

1600 - 1750

The Baroque Era

Characterized by ornate details, complex polyphony, and the rise of tonality. Music was composed for the church and the court, often featuring the harpsichord and organ.

Key Figures: Bach, Handel, Vivaldi
1750 - 1820

The Classical Era

A shift towards clarity, balance, and structural perfection. The piano replaced the harpsichord, and forms like the Symphony and Sonata were standardized.

Key Figures: Haydn, Mozart, Beethoven
1820 - 1900

The Romantic Era

Music became a vehicle for intense personal expression, nationalism, and storytelling. Orchestras grew larger, and harmonies became richer and more dramatic.

Key Figures: Chopin, Tchaikovsky, Wagner
1900 - Present

20th Century & Modern

A time of rebellion against tradition. Composers explored atonality, impressionism, minimalism, and experimental sounds, breaking all previous rules.

Key Figures: Debussy, Stravinsky, Glass
